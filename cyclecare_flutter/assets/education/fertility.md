# Fertility Awareness

Understanding your fertile window can help whether you're trying to conceive or avoid pregnancy.

## Your Fertile Window
You are most fertile in the 5 days before ovulation and the day of ovulation itself. Sperm can survive up to 5 days in the reproductive tract.

## Tracking Methods

### Basal Body Temperature (BBT)
- Take temperature first thing every morning before getting out of bed
- A sustained rise of 0.2°C or more indicates ovulation has occurred
- Track daily for patterns over several cycles

### Cervical Mucus
- **Dry**: Low fertility
- **Sticky**: Transitioning
- **Creamy**: Moderate fertility
- **Watery**: High fertility
- **Egg-white**: Peak fertility (ovulation approaching)

### Cervical Position
The cervix changes position, firmness, and openness throughout the cycle, providing additional fertility clues.
